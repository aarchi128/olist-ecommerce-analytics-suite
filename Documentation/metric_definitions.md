# Business Logic & Metric Formula Formulations

To ensure absolute metrics governance across all engineering, database, and analytical visualization layers, this document outlines the core business rules, mathematical models, and expressions utilized across the platform.

## 🔢 Financial Performance Metrics

These metrics drive the **Executive Financial Overview** and track top-line platform health, velocity, and performance consistency.

### 1. Gross Revenue
The total baseline monetary value generated by processed transactions prior to any external operational deductions.
$$\text{Gross Revenue} = \sum (\text{item\_price} \times \text{quantity\_ordered})$$

* **DAX Implementation:**
```dax
Total Revenue = SUMX('view_order_items', 'view_order_items'[price] * 'view_order_items'[quantity])
```

### 2. Month-over-Month (MoM) Growth Rate
Measures the percentage change and velocity of platform revenue adjustments across consecutive monthly fiscal cycles.
$$\text{MoM Growth Rate} = \frac{\text{Current Month Revenue} - \text{Prior Month Revenue}}{\text{Prior Month Revenue}} \times 100$$

* **DAX Implementation:**
```dax
MoM Growth Rate = 
VAR CurrentMonthRev = [Total Revenue]
VAR PriorMonthRev = CALCULATE([Total Revenue], PARALLELPERIOD('Calendar'[Date], -1, MONTH))
RETURN
DIVIDE(CurrentMonthRev - PriorMonthRev, PriorMonthRev, 0)
```

## 👥 RFM Customer Segmentation Threshold Matrix

Customers are algorithmically assigned a tiered behavioral rank from 1 (lowest) to 5 (highest) across Recency ($R$), Frequency ($F$), and Monetary ($M$) vectors. The combined matrix maps users into distinct actionable cohorts.

| Segment Classification | Core Assignment Criteria | Strategic Business Action |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Champions** | High Recency (5), High Frequency (5), High Monetary (5) | Deploy premium loyalty reward previews, early-access features, and referral incentives. |
| **Loyal Customers** | Stable Recency (3-4), High Frequency (4-5), High Monetary (4-5) | Target with cross-sell campaigns for high-margin items and long-term membership models. |
| **Recent Newbies** | Max Recency (5), Low Frequency (1), Low Monetary (1-2) | Trigger automated onboarding emails, localized introductory discounts, and product feedback prompts. |
| **Can't Lose Them** | Low Recency (1-2), Max Frequency (5), Max Monetary (5) | Treat as critical churn threats. Deploy heavy win-back discounts and immediate customer support outreach. |
| **Lost / Hibernating** | Low Recency (1), Low Frequency (1), Low Monetary (1) | Engage through low-cost automated email recovery flows or deprecate from expensive ad spend pools. |

## 🚛 Supply Chain & Logistical Velocity Metrics

Fulfillment analytics isolate time-based variations within the lifecycle of an order to pin down whether processing delays stem from internal warehouse processing or external third-party shipping carriers.

### 1. Warehouse Handling Time (`days_to_ship`)
Measures internal fulfillment operational efficiency. It tracks the net days elapsed from the exact customer payment timestamp to the moment the carrier scans the parcel out of the facility.
$$\text{Warehouse Handling Time} = \text{Carrier Pickup Date} - \text{Purchase Timestamp}$$

### 2. Road Transit Time (`transit_days`)
Measures external delivery network velocity. It tracks the net days elapsed from the moment the third-party logistics (3PL) provider accepts the parcel to the final delivery signature.
$$\text{Road Transit Time} = \text{Actual Delivery Date} - \text{Carrier Pickup Date}$$

### 3. Total Actual Delivery Time (`total_actual_delivery_days`)
The total end-to-end processing lifecycle from the consumer's perspective.
$$\text{Total Delivery Time} = \text{Warehouse Handling Time} + \text{Road Transit Time}$$

### 4. SLA Deviation Days (`sla_deviation_days`)
Measures strict logistical compliance against the automated arrival estimate promised to the buyer at checkout.
$$\text{SLA Deviation Days} = \text{Actual Customer Delivery Date} - \text{Estimated Delivery Date}$$

> 💡 **Interpretation Rule:**
> * **Positive Value ($>0$):** Delivery breached contractual agreement. Signifies operational delay (e.g., $+5$ indicates a parcel arrived 5 days late).
> * **Negative Value ($<0$):** Delivery exceeded expectations. Signifies an early arrival (e.g., $-2$ indicates a parcel arrived 2 days ahead of schedule).
